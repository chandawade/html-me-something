Wanderlust

(n) a strong desire or urge to travel and explore the world
• A collector of experiences
• Veni Vidi Amavi

A definition and a few words to describe my infinity for traveling. Although I am approaching a milestone birthday, year 2017 is the first time I actually lived life. I discovered exploration is the key to growth. If we were meant to stay in one place, we'd have roots instead of feet. I kick started my travel journey with a roadtrip in January 2017, to the "deep" South. I had the pleasure to help coordinate and arrange a tour of five HBCUs for 10 juniors and senior throughout the state of Mississippi and Tennessee. It was an AMAZING experience. My last week of January 2017 was spent in Oahu, Hawaii. Can you say beautiful. I spent 9 hours on a plane to arrive at pure bliss. As expected, I got leid pon arrival (be very jealous)...one of the best feelings ever. Unfortunately, my week long escapade ended entirely too quick. Before I knew it, I was back on a plane routed to St. Louis. My next adventure presented itself in March, to the Lonestar State - Dallas, TX.I spent a jammed-pack week full of expected, and some unexpected, adventures with my sister. The one experience I didn't expect was a trip to the chiropractor. *Lesson-learned, don't be lazy and sleep in the chair; just blow up the air mattress.* I departed from Dallas with a newly aligned neck. It would be a couple of months before my next trip. The month of May called for a two-day all expenses paid trip to...(drum roll please) ASHEBORO, NC. By all expenses paid, I mean, a business trip. I honestly didn't know what to expect, but Asheboro isn't what I expected. The best part of my trip undoubtably was the Cheerwine Soda (pop). Had I had overpacked, as I tend to do, I would have brought some back with me. My next trip was planned a couple weeks prior to travel with a friend to one of my favorite destinations, Puerto Rico. I spent a week on the island drinking as many Miami Vices and Rum Punches as imaginable, eating numerous variations of mofongos, and of course, Octopus. I hiked in the El Yunque Rainforest, boogie boarded for the first time, and bar-hopped with strangers who are now social media buddies. Following Puerto Rico, I found myself visiting Dallas again. This time, for my birthday. Three short weeks afterwards, I would visit the land of romance - Paris, France. OH PARIS! How my soul misses thee. What an experience. Let's see, while I was there, I was fake proposed to in front of the Arc de Triomphe, journeyed to the top of the Eiffel Tower, cruised the Seine River, visted the Louve, consumed way too many carbs and too much wine, and shopped entirely too much to the point I had to pay over $75 in overweight baggage fees (yikes!). Needless to say, I had a grand time.
